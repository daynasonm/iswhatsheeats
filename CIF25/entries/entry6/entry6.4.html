BOOK

The Catcher in the Rye follows Holden Caulfield, a disillusioned teenager who wanders New York City after being expelled from prep school. Struggling with grief, identity, and the "phoniness" of the adult world, he searches for meaning and connection. Through his cynical yet vulnerable voice, the novel explores adolescence, alienation, and the desire to protect innocence.
