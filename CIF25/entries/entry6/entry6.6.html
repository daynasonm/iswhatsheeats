BOOK

Guns, Germs, and Steel by Jared Diamond explores why some societies developed advanced technology, political systems, and military power faster than others. He argues that geography, access to domesticable plants and animals, and the spread of disease—not intelligence or culture—shaped the fate of civilizations. The book challenges racial and cultural biases by rooting global inequality in environmental factors.
