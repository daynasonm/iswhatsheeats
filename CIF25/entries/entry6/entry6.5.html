BOOK

The Selfish Gene by Richard Dawkins presents the idea that evolution operates at the level of genes, not individuals or species. Genes are portrayed as "selfish" in the sense that they drive behaviors that increase their chances of being passed on. The book also explores how cooperation, altruism, and complex social behaviors can arise from this genetic self-interest.
